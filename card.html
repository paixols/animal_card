Xoloitzcuintle

The breed name comes from the Aztec mythology god Xolotl "he who reigns fire and lightning" commonly depicted as a dog-headed man.

  • Region: Mexico
  • Type: Watchdog
  • Breed age: +3,000 years
  • Variety: Hairless & Coated

The Xoloitzcuintle or Mexican hairless dog is characterized by its duallity, wrinkles, and dental abnormalities along with a primitive temper. It has a sleek body, almond-shaped eyes, large bat-like ears, and a long neck.
